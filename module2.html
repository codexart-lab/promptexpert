What Makes a Great Prompt?

Creating a good prompt is like giving directions to a friend who's never been to your house before. If your directions are clear, specific, and complete, your friend will arrive quickly. If they're vague or confusing, your friend might get lost!

Weak Prompts Are:

  • Vague and unclear
  • Too short or missing details
  • Confusing or contradictory
  • Not specific about what you want
  • Using words the AI might not understand

Strong Prompts Are:

  • Clear and specific
  • Detailed with enough information
  • Well-structured and logical
  • Focused on exactly what you want
  • Using simple, direct language

The 5 Key Ingredients of Great Prompts

  1. Task: Clearly state what you want the AI to do
  2. Context: Provide background information
  3. Details: Include specific requirements
  4. Format: Explain how you want the answer structured
  5. Tone: Describe the style or voice you want

Before & After: Prompt Makeovers

Let's see how we can transform weak prompts into strong ones using our key ingredients!

Example 1: Homework Help

Weak Prompt:

"Help me with my science homework."

Strong Prompt:

"I'm working on my 7th grade science homework about the water cycle. Can you explain the main stages of the water cycle in simple terms? Please include what happens at each stage and why it's important. Also, could you add a fun fact about the water cycle that might surprise me?"

What improved? The strong prompt specifies the topic (water cycle), grade level (7th), format (simple explanations with stages), and asks for additional interesting information (fun fact).

Example 2: Story Creation

Weak Prompt:

"Write me a story."

Strong Prompt:

"Write a short adventure story about a 12-year-old who discovers a secret door in their school library. The story should be exciting but not scary, about 300 words long, and include a surprising twist at the end. Please use simple language that a middle school student would enjoy."

What improved? The strong prompt includes the genre (adventure), main character (12-year-old), setting (school library), tone (exciting but not scary), length (300 words), special elements (twist ending), and target audience (middle school).
